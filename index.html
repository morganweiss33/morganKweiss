Passion for Technology

It all started my sophmore year of high school...

During this time (2014) I started helping my grandparents with their iPhones and iPads they were getting for birthdays and Christmas. This soon turned into weekly visits and progressed to teaching them social media as well. During one of my weekly visits my grandma invited her friend over because she needed help since her family did not live in the area. I started to wonder how many other seniors are out in my community that would benefit from this type of service. I was fortunate with the timing because I had recently been elected to be the president of Key Club at my high school for the upcoming (2014) academic year. I thought about this and knew once I began this position (beginning of my junior year) I would start a program to help seniors in our community with technology and social media and call it “TechConnect”. TechConnect not only provided a much-needed service in our community but it was able to provide community service hours for Key Club members. I had meetings with my advisor and high school principal to get approval, meetings in the community to find a place to host the event, and a meeting with the City Mayor to get the word out and see if he had additional ideas on how to promote it. I reached out to newspapers and ended up being interviewed by local newspapers to promote TechConnect. TechConnect has not only provided tech support for seniors but it has also started friendships, bridged two different generations, and with the repeat visitors each month it is also a place to meet new people. Six years later I am proud to say this program is still running and now occurs twice a month. Over my winter breaks I still attend the January session. This program is what created my spark for technology and what eventually led me to purse my current degree at the University of Dayton. Check out another article here!
